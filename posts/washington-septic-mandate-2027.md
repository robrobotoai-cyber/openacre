# Washington's Septic Mandate (Feb 2027): What Real Estate Agents Need to Know

**February 1, 2027** is coming. And if your clients own homes with septic systems, they need to be ready.

Washington State is implementing **WAC 246-272A-0270**, which will require **septic system inspections for every property transfer** statewide. This isn't optional. This isn't a local rule. **This is state law.**

## What's Changing

### Before Feb 1, 2027
- Septic inspections were required **in some counties** (King, Pierce, Snohomish, etc.)
- Other counties had **no requirement**
- Sellers could dodge inspections in permissive areas

### After Feb 1, 2027
- **ALL properties with septic systems** must have a valid inspection before closing
- Inspection must be **completed within 12 months** of the sale
- Report must be **provided to the buyer**
- Buyer can request repairs or credits

---

## What This Means for You

### For Sellers
1. **Budget $400-$600** for inspection (can't avoid it)
2. **Plan ahead** — don't wait until closing week
3. **Expect repair requests** — buyers will know the system's status
4. **Budget for fixes** — major repairs can kill deals or delay closing

### For Buyers
1. **Inspections are mandatory** — you WILL get the system's real condition
2. **Negotiate power** — failing systems = repair credits or price reductions
3. **No surprises after closing** — you know what you're buying
4. **Plan repairs** — failing systems = known cost before purchase

### For Agents
1. **Educate clients early** — explain the timeline and cost
2. **Budget for inspections** — include in closing estimates
3. **Screen properties** — old systems = likely repair requests
4. **Manage expectations** — "System failure" doesn't kill deals, it just adds negotiation
5. **Timeline matters** — order inspections 4-6 weeks before closing

---

## The Real Impact

### What "System Failure" Means

A failed inspection doesn't automatically kill a deal. It means:
- **Buyer sees the damage** (no surprises later)
- **Seller budgets repair costs** upfront
- **Repairs are negotiated before closing**
- **Buyer gets a warrantied system**

**Example:** $8,000 drain field failure
- Before mandate: Buyer discovers after closing, sues seller
- After mandate: Issue resolved before closing, everyone moves forward

## Inspection Timeline for Agents

**6 weeks before closing:**
- Order septic inspection
- Get report within 2-3 weeks

**4 weeks before closing:**
- Review report with seller
- Identify any repairs needed
- Get repair quotes (if necessary)

**2 weeks before closing:**
- Complete any repairs
- Buyer reviews final report
- Close on schedule

## What Gets Inspected

A standard Washington septic inspection includes:

✅ Tank structural condition
✅ Outlet filter condition
✅ Drain field performance (visual/soil conditions)
✅ Distance from well/property lines
✅ System age and maintenance history
✅ Accessible pipes and distribution box

**Cost:** $300-$600 (varies by county and system type)

---

## Commonly Missed Deadlines

1. **Waiting until week before closing** — inspector fully booked
2. **Not budgeting inspection time** — assumes instant results
3. **Finding major repairs too late** — no time to negotiate or repair
4. **Assuming "old = failure"** — many 30-year-old systems are fine
5. **Ignoring repair estimates** — delaying negotiations

---

## Best Practices

1. **Educate on pre-listing inspection** — sell systems BEFORE listing
2. **Budget 4-6 weeks for process** — inspection + repair negotiation
3. **Use certified inspectors** — avoid disputes over findings
4. **Get multiple repair bids** — if needed
5. **Document everything** — inspection reports stay in transaction file
