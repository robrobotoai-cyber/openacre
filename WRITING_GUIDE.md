# OpenAcre Writing Guide

## Voice & Tone
- **Conversational, not institutional.** Start with a real observation, not a thesis statement.
- **Admit complexity.** Don't oversimplify; explain the actual problem and the tradeoffs.
- **Slightly wry, self-aware.** Use dry humor and acknowledge the obvious tension ("Your contractor recommended annual pumping? Yeah, he's not wrong—for his bank account").
- **Skeptical of narratives.** Question assumptions. Show *why* the conventional wisdom exists (often because someone's making money off it).
- **Practical, not theoretical.** Explain what actually happens in the real world, not what regulations or best practices say *should* happen.
- **Use specifics and examples.** Real costs, real code language, real failure stories. Avoid generic advice.

## Structure
- **Lead with a contradiction or real observation** (not a question; not a listicle headline).
  - ❌ "5 Signs Your Septic System Is Failing"
  - ✅ "Your septic system is failing right now, and you probably don't know it—because the early signs don't feel like an emergency."

- **Develop the idea through narrative and examples**, not bullet points.
- **Use subheads to break up the text**, but let the logic flow naturally.
- **End with practical takeaway** (what the reader should actually do, or what they should be skeptical of).

## Forbidden Constructions
- ❌ "It's important to..."
- ❌ "In today's world..."
- ❌ "Utilize" (use "use")
- ❌ "As mentioned above" / "As noted earlier"
- ❌ "The bottom line is..."
- ❌ Lists that could be paragraphs

## Tone Examples from Reference
- "Yay? Look, the timing isn't great."
- "I often say around here, 'everything is securities fraud.'"
- "The obvious answer is [X], but [Y] complicates it."
- "Maybe that's right. But there are two general points here."

## Length & Pace
- **Blog posts should be 1,500–2,500 words.**
- **Vary sentence length.** Short sentences land harder. Long sentences explain complexity.
- **Paragraph length: 2–4 sentences typically.** Break it up.

## Application to Septic Content
1. **Start with a real tension:** Contractors want annual pumping. Science says every 3–5 years. Why the difference? Follow the money.
2. **Explain the actual mechanism:** How septic systems work, why the timeline matters, what the contractor's incentive is.
3. **Admit the nuance:** "In some cases, annual pumping makes sense. Here's when."
4. **Give the reader something to do or think about:** "Ask your contractor to show you the scum layer. If he won't, that's a red flag."

## Example Opening (What We're Aiming For)
❌ **Bad:**
"Septic pumping is one of the most important maintenance tasks homeowners face. In this guide, we'll explore how often you should pump your septic tank and why."

✅ **Good:**
"Your septic contractor probably told you to pump every year. He's not wrong—for his bottom line. But the science says something different, and understanding why could save you thousands."
